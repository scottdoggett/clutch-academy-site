============================================
Last updated: May 13, 2026

Hey Sam — this is the plain-English version of what's happening with

WHAT IS SEO, IN ONE SENTENCE?
-----------------------------
SEO is the work that gets your business to show up when people search
for what you sell. For Clutch, that's "manual driving lessons toronto"
and similar phrases — in Google, in Bing, and increasingly in AI
assistants like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity.


WHAT WE'VE DONE FOR YOU (THE HIGHLIGHTS)
========================================

1. MADE THE SITE READABLE TO GOOGLE AND AI ASSISTANTS
   Most modern websites are tricky for search engines to read. We
   built yours so Google, Bing, ChatGPT, Claude, and the others can
   all see the full content (your services, prices, FAQ, contact info)
   even if they don't run any JavaScript. This is the single biggest
   thing we did and it's invisible to you.

2. CONNECTED YOUR WEBSITE TO YOUR GOOGLE BUSINESS PROFILE
   When someone Googles "Clutch Academy", your Maps/Business listing
   and your website are now officially linked. Google understands
   they're the same business. This helps you appear in the "local
   3-pack" (the map results that show up at the top of local
   searches) and gives AI assistants confidence to recommend you.

3. LISTED YOUR BUSINESS INFO IN A FORMAT GOOGLE LOVES
   Behind the scenes we tell Google things like:
   - You're a driving school in Toronto
   - You offer two packages: $90 single lesson, $240 3-pack
   - Sam Anthony is the founder/instructor
   - Your phone, email, Instagram, Facebook, and Google Business
   - Your full FAQ (so individual questions can show up directly in
     Google's search results)

4. SUBMITTED YOUR SITE TO GOOGLE AND BING
   We registered your site with both, told them where every page
   lives, and asked them to start indexing it. They'll re-check the
   site periodically and update what they show in search results.

5. HOOKED UP GOOGLE ANALYTICS
   Every visitor (who agrees to the cookie banner) is now counted.
   You can see how many people are visiting, where they came from
   (Instagram, Google, direct typing, etc.), what pages they look
   at, and which "Book Now" buttons they click.

6. HOOKED UP META PIXEL (FOR YOUR FACEBOOK/INSTAGRAM ADS)
   Same idea but for Meta. When you run ads, Meta can see which ad
   clicks turned into someone visiting your site, clicking Book Now,
   or contacting you. This makes your ad spend more efficient
   because Meta learns which audiences actually convert.

7. SET UP A COOKIE CONSENT BANNER
   When a visitor first arrives, they get a small popup asking if
   they're OK with tracking. Nothing fires until they accept. This
   keeps you compliant with Canadian and European privacy laws.

8. OPTIMIZED EVERYTHING FOR PHONES
   Loads fast on mobile, works on iPhone/Android, the icon looks
   right when someone adds the site to their iPhone home screen,
   and the photos are now smaller files so the site loads faster
   (good for SEO — Google ranks fast sites higher).

9. MADE THE BRAND ICON CONSISTENT
   The little icon you see in the browser tab and next to your
   website in Google search results is now the new Clutch logo.


THINGS YOU SHOULD DO YOURSELF
=============================

A. ASK YOUR STUDENTS FOR GOOGLE REVIEWS
   This is THE highest-leverage thing you can do. Reviews on your
   Google Business Profile:
   - Push you higher in local search results
   - Let us eventually add a star-rating to your website that shows
     up directly in Google results (we can't do this until you have
     real reviews)
   - Influence whether ChatGPT/Claude recommends you
   How: After every lesson, text the student a link to your Google
   review page. Make it dead simple — most won't bother if they have
   to find your business themselves.

B. CONFIRM "hello@clutchacademy.ca" ACTUALLY RECEIVES EMAIL
   We updated the contact email everywhere on the site to
   "hello@clutchacademy.ca" because it looks more professional than
   a personal address. BUT — you need to make sure this email
   address actually works. Log into your email provider (Google
   Workspace, GoDaddy, wherever your email is hosted) and either
   create the inbox or set up "hello@" as an alias that forwards to
   your main email. Otherwise inquiries will bounce.

C. KEEP POSTING ON INSTAGRAM AND FACEBOOK
   Both are linked from your site, which strengthens your overall
   web presence. Google notices when a business has active social
   accounts that point back to its website.

D. SEARCH YOURSELF EVERY COUPLE WEEKS
   In an incognito/private window (so you see what a stranger sees):
   - Google: "manual driving lessons toronto" — do you appear in
     the map results at the top?
   - Google: "clutch academy" — does your website appear first?
   - ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity/Gemini: "where can I learn manual
     transmission in Toronto?" — do you get mentioned?
   This is how you know SEO is working. If you don't show up, tell
   Scott so he can investigate.

E. SHARE YOUR WEBSITE LINK EVERYWHERE
   Every time another website mentions "clutchacademy.ca", it's a
   signal to Google that you're real and reputable. Some easy wins:
   - Your Instagram bio
   - Your Facebook page "About" section
   - Email signature
   - Any directory listings (Yelp, local Toronto business
     directories, driving school review sites)


WHAT TO TRACK — THE FOUR DASHBOARDS
====================================

You can check these whenever you want — weekly is a good rhythm
once ads are running.


1. GOOGLE ANALYTICS — "Who's visiting my site?"
   URL: https://analytics.google.com

   What to look at:
   - Reports → Realtime — see if anyone is on the site RIGHT NOW
     (good for verifying it's working after a change)
   - Reports → Acquisition → Traffic acquisition — where are
     visitors coming from? Google, Instagram, ads, direct?
   - Reports → Engagement → Events — look for "booking_cta_click"
     to see how often people click any Book Now button. The
     "source" tells you which Book Now button (hero, packages,
     about section, etc.)

   What to actually care about:
   - Total visitors per week (trending up?)
   - What percentage of visitors click Book Now
   - Which traffic source (Instagram vs Google vs ads) converts best


2. GOOGLE BUSINESS PROFILE INSIGHTS — "How many people found my GBP?"
   URL: https://business.google.com

   What to look at:
   - Performance tab — searches that showed your business
   - "Calls", "Directions", "Website clicks" counts

   What to actually care about:
   - Are searches for "manual driving lessons toronto" showing
     your business?
   - Are people calling/visiting your website from the GBP card?
   - Reviews count — when this number grows, your local SEO improves


3. META EVENTS MANAGER — "Are my Facebook/Instagram ads working?"
   URL: https://business.facebook.com → Events Manager →
        dataset 2845684255788584

   What to look at (especially after launching ads):
   - Test Events tab — paste your live URL, click around, confirm
     the events PageView, Lead, Contact are all firing
   - Overview — see total event counts over the last 7/28 days

   What to actually care about:
   - Cost per Lead (Meta Ads Manager) — what does it cost in ad
     spend to get someone to click Book Now?
   - "Lead" events should match the number of people opening
     Calendly from the site

   IMPORTANT: Your Calendly is already linked to Meta separately
   and fires a "Schedule" event when someone actually books. The
   website does NOT fire a duplicate "Schedule" event because that
   would inflate your stats. So:
   - "Lead" = clicked Book Now (website tracks this)
   - "Schedule" = actually booked (Calendly tracks this)
   The combination of the two tells you "click-to-book" conversion.


4. GOOGLE SEARCH CONSOLE — "What are people searching to find me?"
   URL: https://search.google.com/search-console

   What to look at:
   - Performance → Search results — see what search queries are
     showing your site, how often you appear, and how often people
     click through
   - Indexing → Pages — confirms Google has crawled and indexed
     your homepage

   What to actually care about:
   - Which queries you're appearing for (and which you'd LIKE to
     appear for but aren't)
   - Average position (lower number is better — position 1 is the
     top result)
   - Total clicks per month

WHAT TO EXPECT, TIMELINE-WISE
=============================

- IMMEDIATE: Site works, ads can run, Analytics records every visit
- 1-2 WEEKS: Google finishes its first full crawl, your site
  reliably appears for "clutch academy" searches
- 2-4 WEEKS: Local pack appearance for "manual driving lessons
  toronto" should solidify (depends partly on your GBP activity
  and any new reviews)
- 1-3 MONTHS: AI assistants (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) update their
  data and start mentioning Clutch as a Toronto manual-driving
  option

SEO is a slow burn. Don't worry if you don't see massive movement
in the first few weeks — what matters is that the foundation is
solid and you're slowly building up trust signals (reviews, social
posts, third-party mentions, returning visitors).
