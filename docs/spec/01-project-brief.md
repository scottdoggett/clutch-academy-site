# 01 — Project Brief

## Business Overview

**Name:** Clutch Academy
**Service:** One-on-one manual transmission driving lessons
**Location:** Toronto, Ontario (exact lesson location TBD — see `05-pending-items.md`)
**Business model:** Single instructor teaching licensed drivers how to operate a stick-shift vehicle

## Primary Goals (in priority order)

The site must accomplish three things, ordered by importance:

1. **Drive bookings via Calendly.** Every section should ultimately funnel toward the Book Now CTA.
2. **Build trust.** Prospects are handing their learning experience to a stranger. The About section and testimonials carry this weight.
3. **Communicate packages and pricing clearly.** Transparent pricing is a differentiator vs. consultation-based competitors.

## Target Audience

 **Demographics:** Licensed G2 or G drivers in the Greater Toronto Area, ages 17–35, skewing male but not exclusively.

### Motivations (in order of emphasis)

1. **Renting a manual car on a European summer trip** — this was specifically flagged to emphasize. Many European rental cars are manual-only or significantly cheaper in manual. This should show up in copy and imagery cues.
2. Enthusiast interest (car culture, sports cars)
3. Owning or planning to own a manual car
4. Learning the skill for its own sake / resilience

### Audience DOES include

- G2/G licensed drivers who have never touched a manual
- Drivers who learned on automatic and want to refresh or formalize manual skills
- Travelers preparing for Europe

### Audience does NOT include

- Unlicensed learners (MTO-certified beginner lessons are explicitly not offered)
- Fleet/commercial drivers
- Minors without a license

## Positioning

The positioning angle is **instructor-led** — patience, professionalism, and one-on-one attention are the hooks. This follows the pattern of competitors like Shifters (Carlos) and StickGTA (Stan), where the instructor's personality carries the brand.

**Implication for the build:** The About section must be prominent, personal, and photo-forward.

## Competitive Context

Clutch Academy operates in a small but established GTA market. Competitors include:

| Competitor | Angle | Pricing |
|---|---|---|
| DrivingMan (Mississauga) | Mustang GT + Calendly | $100/hr, $275/3hr |
| StickGTA (Toronto) | High volume, reviews, gift certs | $85 / $110 / $135 |
| Shifters (Etobicoke) | Premium coaching, dual-control car | Consultation-based |
| Race Lab (Thornhill) | Motorsport curriculum, rally fleet | $319–$459 / 2hr |

Clutch Academy's pricing ($90/hr, $240/3-pack) positions it competitively against StickGTA and DrivingMan — mid-market, accessible, clear.

## What Makes Clutch Academy Different

This is the core positioning to reinforce in content (awaiting final voice from client):

- One-on-one patient instruction
- Real-road practice, not just parking lots
- Transparent upfront pricing
- Toronto-based (vs. suburban competitors)
